PLAYER GOALS QUESTIONNAIRE

There are no right or wrong answers. Answer honestly — the goal of this questionnaire is to help coaches understand who you are as a player and teammate, and to start a real conversation about what this team means to each of you.

01
The coach reduces your playing time to give other players more opportunity. What is your immediate reaction?
02
In a close game, you're in a good position to shoot. A teammate is in a slightly better position. What do you do?
03
Your team loses a big game. You personally played well. What is your honest feeling when you walk off the field?
04
A teammate is going through a rough stretch — poor form, missing sessions, seems checked out. What do you do?
05
What is your most important personal goal for this season?
06
You make a costly mistake that leads directly to the other team scoring. What do you do?
07
The coaching staff organizes voluntary training sessions over winter break. What do you do?
08
Your team is winning 4–0 and the coach is rotating players. You've played 20 minutes. How do you feel?
09
The coach makes a tactical decision you strongly disagree with. What do you do?
10
A teammate calls you out in front of the team for something they feel you did wrong. How do you respond?
11
You and a teammate have personal tension that's creating friction in the squad. What do you do?
12
Honestly — when you picture a successful season for yourself, what does it look like?
Answer all 12 questions before submitting.
